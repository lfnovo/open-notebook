What do my sources agree on about rebuilding focus?
Notebook

All five sources converge on one premise: attention is a trainable, finite resource — not a fixed trait. Hari argues that environment beats willpower, so the first lever is removing frictionless distraction rather than resisting it Stolen Focus. Newport quantifies why: every context switch leaves an "attention residue" that taxes the next task, which is what makes fragmented work so expensive Newport.

The video completes the picture from the supply side — the distraction isn't accidental, it's a business model optimized against your focus How Your Attention Is Sold. The practical agreement: design your environment, protect long single-task blocks, and treat concentration as a capacity you rebuild through deliberate practice.
