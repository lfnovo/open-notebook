What do my sources agree on about rebuilding focus?
Notebook

All five sources converge on one premise: attention is a finite, trainable resource — not a character trait. Hari argues the decisive move is environmental, not heroic: change what surrounds you before asking more of your willpower 2, and Newport puts a number on the damage, showing that each context switch leaves a residue that taxes the next block of work 3.

The video closes the loop from the supply side: the feeds you return to are engineered by a business model that profits precisely from those interruptions 1. Where they agree in practice — protect long, unbroken blocks, and treat rebuilding concentration as training, done in deliberate, repeated sessions.
