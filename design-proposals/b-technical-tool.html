What do my sources agree on about rebuilding focus?
assistant14:02 · 3.1s

Across all five sources there is one clear point of convergence: attention is a finite, trainable resource — not a fixed personality trait. Hari's argument is that environment beats willpower: as long as the inputs around you are engineered to interrupt, no amount of discipline compensates [src:2]. Newport quantifies the same mechanism from the cost side, showing that each context switch leaves a residue that degrades the next block of work [src:3].

The video closes the loop on incentives: the distraction isn't accidental, it's the business model — your focus is the inventory being sold [src:1]. Where the sources diverge is remedy, not diagnosis: Hari argues for changing the environment, Newport for scheduling depth deliberately. Both converge on the same practical lever — protect long, uninterrupted blocks first, and treat concentration as something you rebuild through repetition.
