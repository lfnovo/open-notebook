What do my sources agree on about rebuilding focus?
ON

Across all five, one idea keeps surfacing: attention is a finite, trainable resource — not a fixed personality trait. Hari's strongest claim is that environment beats willpower: you don't out-discipline a feed engineered against you, you redesign the room. The video makes the same point from the supply side — as long as your attention is the product being sold, the defaults will never be on your side.

Newport puts numbers on the damage: every context switch leaves attention residue that degrades the next block of work, which is why his fix — and the podcast's rebuilding protocol — is long, scheduled stretches of single focus rather than scattered "productive" minutes. Even the arXiv paper rhymes: selective attention is the whole mechanism — what you attend to determines what gets processed at all.
